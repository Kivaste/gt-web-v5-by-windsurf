Everybody loves cookies! (until they follow you) üç™

Not the chocolate‚Äëchip kind. Cookies are tiny text crumbs your browser saves so sites can remember things about you. Handy‚Ä¶ and also a little clingy.

  • First‚Äëparty cookies: set by the site you're on ‚Äî logins, carts, "remember me," that sort of useful stuff.
  • Third‚Äëparty cookies: set by outsiders embedded on the page (ad networks, social widgets, analytics). That's how the same folks can spot you across many sites.
  • Session vs. persistent: session cookies vanish when you close the tab; persistent cookies stick around for days or months, like glitter after a craft accident.

Retargeting in 4 steps (aka "why am I seeing those exact shoes?")

A/B Testing: stop guessing, start splitting

Marketing opinions are free. Evidence is expensive. Here's the cheap version:

  • Pick one thing to mess with: headline, button text, price anchoring, whatever.
  • Randomly show A or B to visitors. No peeking at the results "just to check."
  • Measure one clear metric (clicks, signups, buys). Not "feelings."
  • Let it run long enough to matter. "We got 12 clicks" is not a conclusion.
  • Winner goes live. Loser goes to the big archive in the sky.
Serious A/B requires tooling or backend logic. This is your heads‚Äëup that "best practices" are just hypotheses with a good PR team.
